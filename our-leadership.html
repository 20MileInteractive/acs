Rebecca Pries, LMHC, CJCC I Mentor Executive Director

Rebecca has practiced as a mental health counselor, evaluating and treating acting-out teens and their families for over 30 years. She oversees all aspects of ACS.

Rebecca Pries, EdM, CAGS, LMHC, serves as Executive Director of Adolescent Consultation Services (ACS), a private, non-profit agency which operates the Juvenile Court Clinics for the Middlesex County Juvenile Courts and provides treatment services for court-involved children and families. Rebecca has practiced as a licensed mental health counselor, evaluating and treating acting-out teens and their families for over 30 years. She has taught and supervised child psychiatry fellows and psychology interns and has presented at Harvard Law School, Harvard Medical School Conferences, and professional conferences. Her early research interest was in the interrelationship between learning disabilities and delinquency. Most recently she has co-authored the book, Kids and the Law: A User's Guide to the Court System, a practical guide to laws and court practices that affect juveniles.

Dan Sanford Clinical Director

Dan Sanford, PsyD, Clinical Director. Dan is a licensed psychologist with a specialty in forensic evaluations including competency to stand trial and criminal responsibility evaluations. As Clinical Director he oversees all clinical aspects of ACS services. Dan is an adjunct faculty member at Tufts Medical School and Massachusetts School of Professional Psychology. He is a CJCC I and CJCC II Mentor.
