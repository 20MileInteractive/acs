Who We Are

ACS clinicians are what make ACS unique. Juvenile Court Clinicians provide emergency and comprehensive evaluations that take into account biological, psychological and social factors of some of the most vulnerable children involved with the Juvenile Court. All Juvenile Court Clinicians are certified* and specially trained in the issues impacting court-involved youth and families including complex trauma, mental health diagnoses, substance abuse, learning disabilities and cultural issues.

* The Certified Juvenile Court Clinician (CJCC) designation has two levels: Level I clinicians are licensed masters degree social workers or mental health counselors who conduct CRA, Delinquency, and Care and Protection evaluations; Level II clinicians are licensed clinicians with doctoral degrees in psychology or psychiatry who provide Competency to Stand Trial, Criminal Responsibility, and Section 35 Substance Abuse evaluations. Clinicians with further training and experience are credentialed as Mentors. Certification is provided through the Department of Mental Health Forensic Division in conjunction with University of Massachusetts Medical School.
